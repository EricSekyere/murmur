# The floating pill

The floating pill is a small always on top window that shows Murmur's status and
acts as a quick mic button. It stays visible over other apps so you can dictate
from anywhere.

## What the pill states mean

The pill is your status light. It shows when Murmur is idle and ready, when it is
listening and recording your voice, and when it is thinking (transcribing what
you just said). Watching the pill tells you at a glance whether your speech is
being captured.

If you pause speaking for a few seconds mid-session, the pill dims and its label
reads "waiting". That is not off: the session is still live and dictation resumes
automatically the moment you speak again. The session only ends for real after
the Session Timeout of total inactivity (or when you stop it yourself).

## Move the pill

Drag the pill anywhere on screen with your mouse. It remembers where you put it.
If you use multiple monitors, you can park it wherever it stays out of your way.

## Find the pill

If you lose the pill behind other windows or drag it off screen, open the Dictate
view and click Find pill. This flashes the pill and pulls it back onto your
primary monitor if it has drifted off every display.

## Hide the pill

Turn off Floating Widget in Settings to hide the pill entirely. You can still
dictate with your hotkey or double-tap key; you just lose the on-screen status
light and the quick mic button.

## Live preview caption

When Live Preview is on, your words appear on screen as you speak, before the
phrase is final. The caption can sit directly under the pill, or near the bottom
of the window you are dictating into. Choose which under Settings as the Live
caption position. Each caption clears once its phrase is delivered.

## Live preview and latency

Live preview does a little extra work per session to show interim text. If you
want the absolute lowest latency, turn Live Preview off in Settings and your
words will appear only when each phrase is finalized.
