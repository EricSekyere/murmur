# Getting started

Murmur turns your voice into text in any app. Hold or press your hotkey, speak,
and the words are typed wherever your cursor is.

## Dictate with the hotkey

The default hotkey is Ctrl+Shift+Space. Press it to start a session, speak, then
press it again to stop (or just pause, depending on your activation mode). The
floating pill shows the current state: idle, listening, recording, or thinking.

## Change your hotkey

Open Settings, find Activation, and use Change next to Global hotkey to record a
new shortcut. If a hotkey fails to register, another app is usually already using
it; pick a different combination.

## Push to talk vs toggle

In toggle mode one press starts recording and the next stops it. In push to talk
(hold) mode, recording lasts only while you hold the key. Set this under
Activation as the activation mode.

## Where your text goes

Text is delivered to the window that was focused when you started the session, so
switching windows mid sentence will not send your words to the wrong place. On a
terminal it is pasted; elsewhere it is typed as keystrokes.

## The floating pill

The small always on top pill is your status light and a quick mic button. Drag it
anywhere. If you lose it behind other windows, use Find pill on the Dictate view
to flash it.
