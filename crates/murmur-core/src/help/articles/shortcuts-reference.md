# Shortcuts and commands reference

A quick reference to Murmur's activation keys and spoken commands. Activation is
configurable in Settings; the spoken commands are fixed.

## Default activation

The default global hotkey is Ctrl+Shift+Space on Windows and Linux, and
Super+Shift+Space on macOS. The default double-tap key is Right Ctrl on Windows
(either Ctrl on macOS and Linux). Both are configurable in Settings.

## Start and stop

In toggle mode, press the hotkey (or double-tap the activation key) to start, and
again to stop. In hold (push to talk) mode, recording lasts only while you hold
the key. A short silence after you speak can also auto-stop the session.

## Spoken line break commands

Say "new line" for a single line break and "new paragraph" for a blank line.
These fire only when spoken as the whole phrase.

## Spoken editing commands

Say "scratch that" or "delete that" to remove your last phrase, "undo that" to
undo, "redo that" to redo, and "copy that" or "copy selection" to copy. The
two-word forms of undo and redo are required to avoid misfires.

## Spoken key presses

Say "press tab" or "tab key" to press Tab, and "press escape" or "escape key" to
press Escape.

## Literal escape

Prefix a command or snippet with "literally" or "literal" to type its words
instead of running it, for example "literally new line".

## Not voice commands

Paste, cut, and select all are intentionally not voice triggered, because a single
misrecognition could be destructive. Saying them types the words instead.

## App buttons

On the Dictate view, the mic button starts and stops a session, Find pill flashes
the floating pill, and the floating pill itself doubles as a mic button you can
click from anywhere.
